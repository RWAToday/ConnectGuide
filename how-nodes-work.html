How nodes work

BlockBot and Droid: what they are, what they do, and how rewards work

A node on the WIN Blockchain is not a server you manage yourself. It is a participation product that runs on the network and earns rewards based on verified action — not passive holding.

What is a node? BlockBot vs Droid Proof of Action How rewards work Verify on-chain FAQ

What is a node on the WIN Blockchain?

In most blockchain networks, a "node" is a computer that validates transactions and maintains a copy of the ledger. On the WIN Blockchain, a BlockBot or Droid node connects your account to the blockchain — and that connection is required to earn. An offline node earns nothing. Nodes must be actively hosted and online to participate in Proof of Action rewards.

You have two options for hosting your node:

  • Self-hosted — run the node software on your own hardware or a VPS (Virtual Private Server) that you manage yourself
  • Codex as a service — use Codex, a managed hosting service within the Connect ecosystem, which handles the technical setup and keeps your node online without you managing the infrastructure directly

Either way, your node's connection to the WIN Blockchain is what enables you to earn from Learn2Earn completions, Give2Earn, and other Proof of Action activities.

Verify node activity: Block production, wallet counts, and transaction activity on the WIN Blockchain are publicly visible at win.elevatescan.com. You do not need a node to view this data.

BlockBot vs Droid: what is the difference?

Both products operate on the WIN Blockchain and participate in the Proof of Action reward model. The differences are in access level and governance rights:

⚡

BlockBot

The primary node tier. BlockBot owners receive weekly Learn2Earn access — a new lesson every seven days. They hold full governance voting rights under the Distributed Governance Framework Charter and vote on reward parameters and community pool allocations.

🤖

Droid

The entry-level node option. Droid owners receive monthly Learn2Earn access — one lesson per month. Droids participate in the Proof of Action reward model. Governance rights may differ from BlockBot — check the current DGF Charter at win.node-governance.io for specifics.

What is Proof of Action?

Proof of Action (PoA) is the WIN Blockchain's consensus and reward model. Unlike Proof of Work (which rewards computational power) or Proof of Stake (which rewards token holdings), Proof of Action rewards demonstrated participation.

In practice, this means: to earn rewards, you must complete verified actions. The most common action is the Learn2Earn cycle — watch the lesson, pass the quiz, complete the action step, submit verification. This produces an on-chain NFT credential and earns the associated reward.

The intent behind Proof of Action is to prevent passive wealth accumulation from concentrated token holdings. Larger holdings do not automatically earn more — participation does.

How are rewards calculated and distributed?

Reward parameters on the WIN Blockchain are set by node owner governance votes through the DGF — not by Connect United's management unilaterally. The WIN Blockchain itself distributes rewards directly to node owner wallets; Connect United does not control or intermediate the reward process. The current parameters at any time are publicly visible in governance records at win.node-governance.io.

Key facts about the reward structure:

  • WIN has a fixed maximum supply of 50 billion tokens — no inflationary issuance beyond this cap
  • Built-in halving mechanisms reduce emission rates over time, similar in principle to Bitcoin's halving schedule
  • Halving schedule changes require a governance vote — they cannot be changed unilaterally
  • Rewards are distributed in WIN tokens to node owner wallets
